# Code of Conduct

This project covers real companies, real failures, and sometimes real people who made real
mistakes. That calls for a specific kind of care, on top of the basics.

## The basics

Be respectful. Disagree about content, sourcing, or approach without attacking the person you're
disagreeing with. Harassment, personal attacks, and discriminatory language aren't welcome here.

## Specific to this project

- **Be fair to the subjects of case studies.** The goal is to analyze failure mechanisms, not to
  humiliate people or companies. Keep language factual and sourced — see
  [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) and [LEGAL.md](LEGAL.md) for the sourcing and
  allegation-vs-conviction standards this project holds itself to.
- **Assume good faith on corrections.** If someone (including a subject of a case, or their
  representative) requests a correction, engage with the substance of the claim, not the
  messenger. See LEGAL.md for how correction requests are handled.
- **No brigading or dogpiling** on a real company or individual beyond what the sourced facts
  support, in issues, PRs, or discussion here.

## Reporting

If you see conduct that doesn't fit this, [open an issue](https://github.com/DOTfei/Learn-From-Failure/issues/new)
or contact a maintainer directly through GitHub. Reports are handled discreetly.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, remove, or reject contributions, and may block contributors, for conduct
that violates this document, at their discretion.
